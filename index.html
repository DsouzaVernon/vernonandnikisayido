The Grand march 8 - 8:30pm

This wedding tradition originated in Poland and spread all over the world. The steps to the dance as not always the same, but the general steps go as follows:

  1. The dance begins with the bride and groom being led around the room in a circle followed by other couples. This part signifies starting wedding life and growing up.
  2. The couple separate during the dance, signifying a quarrel in the relationship.
  3. The couple join together in rows with family and friends to signify the growing family.
  4. The dance then returns to single file line. This forms a snake to signify the twists and turns of life the couple with have in life.
  5. The couples then join together to form a bridge to which each person goes under to signify the strength of the new couples bond.
  6. The couple then joins back together to signify making-up.
  7. At the end of the dance, the newlyweds dance together while the wedding party and guests form a circle around them. This signifies the support of the family in marriage.

Sado dressing | Food counter & Dance Floor open 9 - 9:30pm

The Mangalorean Catholic bride's wedding sari (drape) is known as sado. It is usually a red coloured Benaresi sari, made of finely woven silk, decorated with elaborate gold embroidery called zàri (brocade). In olden days, the bride wore on her head a red cloth, three feet long and as many broad.

Travel Tips

Apps to Download

  1. Getting around: We recommend downloading the Ola and Rapido apps for affordable taxis and rickshaws around the city. You’ll likely need an Indian e-SIM to register on these apps. Please note that Uber's pricing has not been updated, so most local taxi services no longer use it
  2. Revolut or Google Pay: Some stores can have issues with accepting cards, so it's advisable to also carry cash
  3. Whatsapp: For communication
  4. Google Maps: For saving our map from Glimpse of the city and for general travel in India
  5. Google Translate: While most people speak English, it’s a good idea to download Kannada on Google Translate for added convenience and to ensure smooth communication

Want to make a Longer Trip?

  1. Goa: Goa is a perfect blend of stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage, making it an ideal getaway for relaxation and adventure alike
  2. Bangalore: Often referred to as the 'Silicon Valley of India,' Bangalore is not just about business. It also offers a vibrant scene for craft beer and cocktail enthusiasts, making it a great destination for those looking to unwind
  3. Kerala: Kerala, known as 'God's Own Country,' offers a serene escape with its lush landscapes, tranquil backwaters, and rich cultural experiences
